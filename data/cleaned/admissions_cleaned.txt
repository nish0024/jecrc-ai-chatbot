### B.Tech Admission Eligibility & Scholarship Guidelines

#### Admission Eligibility (B.Tech)
* **Academic Requirement:** Minimum **60% aggregate** in 10+2 (Senior Secondary).
* **Mandatory Subjects:** English, Physics, and Mathematics.
* **Optional Subjects (One of):** Chemistry, Biology, Biotechnology, or a Technical Vocational subject.
* **Selection Preference:** Valid **JEE (Main)** scores are prioritized.
* **Category Relaxations:** A **5% relaxation** (55% minimum) is granted to SC, ST, Rajasthan OBC (Non-creamy layer), and Women candidates.

---

#### Scholarship Schemes
Scholarships apply to the **Tuition Fee** only. Students can only avail of one scholarship at a time (highest applicable).

| Scholarship Type | Criteria (10+2 Aggregate) | Applicable Programs | Amount |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Merit Scholarship** | 70% to 84.99% | All UG Programs | 15% |
| **Merit Scholarship** | 85% and Above | All UG Programs | 25% |
| **Additional Category** | NCC 'A', Alumni, Sibling, Single Mother/Girl, Sports Medalist, etc. | All Programs | 10% (One-time) |

---

#### Scholarship Maintenance & Continuation
To retain a scholarship in subsequent years, students must strictly adhere to the following:
* **Academic Performance:** Maintain a minimum **8.0 CGPA** (SGPA in the 1st semester).
* **Attendance:** Minimum **75% attendance** in every individual subject (Theory/Lab/Project).
* **Clean Record:** No "compartments" (failed subjects) and no disciplinary actions/punishments.
* **Review Cycle:** Scholarships are reviewed annually at the end of even semesters.

> **Important Note on Restoration:** If a scholarship is withdrawn due to low grades or attendance, it may be restored after the next review cycle if the student meets the 8.0 CGPA and 75% attendance criteria. However, if withdrawn for **indiscipline**, it will never be restored.

---

#### Key Restrictions
* **No Rounding Off:** A CGPA of 7.99 or attendance of 75% (if the requirement is >75%) will result in immediate forfeiture.
* **No External Aid:** Students receiving financial assistance from the Government or private organizations are ineligible for JECRC scholarships.
* **Discretion:** The University reserves the right to discontinue scholarship schemes or make final awarding decisions.
